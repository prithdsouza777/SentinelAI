### Where SentinelAI Wins Decisively
1. **Demo drama** — The 3-minute arc (Calm → Storm → Negotiation → Resolution → Intelligence) is the best story in the room. No other team has a choreographed incident scenario.
2. **Multi-Agent Negotiation Protocol** — Unique. No other team has AI agents competing, conflicting, and resolving. This is the moment judges remember.
3. **Simulation Engine** — Zero dependency on live AWS. Other teams with real integrations risk live demo failures. ChatGPT flagged "hard to simulate realistic queue data" as a risk for SentinelAI — it is NOT a risk because the simulation engine is fully built.
4. **AI Governance Overlay** — Confidence scores (0–1), approval gates, human-in-the-loop, audit trail. This is the enterprise credibility card judges from consulting backgrounds will value.
5. **"What just happened?" chat recap** — The intelligence finale. Conversational AI summarizing a live incident is a stunning closing moment.
6. **Cost Impact Ticker** — Real-time dollar accumulation during the storm is viscerally effective.

### SentinelAI's Key Risks
1. **Backend at 0%**: Simulation loop, all agents, all routes are still stubs. Everything depends on W1-W3 executing successfully.
2. **Demo timing**: 3-minute script is tight. If The Storm segment overruns, The Intelligence (chat) gets cut — one of the most impressive parts.
3. **Narrow domain**: Contact center only. Teams 3 and 7 have "everyone's problem" narratives. SentinelAI needs to sell hard on ROI for contact centers ($1,240 saved in demo scenario).
4. **Complexity perception**: Judges might perceive "4 agents + negotiation + governance" as over-engineered. Counter: show it working flawlessly in 3 minutes.
